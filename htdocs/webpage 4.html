Some Sexual Orientations and Gender Identities

LGBTQIA+ is an abbreviation for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer or questioning, intersex, asexual, and more. These terms are used to describe a person's sexual orientation or gender identity. Examples are as follows.

Lesbian

Lesbian refers to a woman who is physically, romantically, and/or emotionally attracted to nonmen.

Gay

Though commonly used as a term that refers to people who are attracted to someone who is not of the opposite sex or someone who is attracted to both the opposite sex and not of the opposite sex, the term "Gay" actually is used to refer to a man who is physically, romantically, and/or emotionally attracted to men.

Bisexual

Bisexual refers to individuals who are attracted to two or more sex, regardless of it being the opposite gender or not.

Transgender

Transgender refers to people whose gender identity and/or gender expression differs to what is typically associated with the sex they were assigned at birth. People under the transgender umbrella may use one or more terms of a wide varietyâ€”such as transgender, nonbinary, or genderfluid.

Queer or Questioning

The term "Queer" is used for someone whose sexual orientation isn't heterosexual or straight. Questioning can also be used and it is a term used for someone who is questioning their sexual orientation or gender identity.

Intersex

A term for someone who has innate sex characteristics, including genitals, internal reproductive organs, chromosomes, or even hormones, that fall outside of the typical conceptions of male or female bodies.

Asexual

Asexual is a term that describes a person who lacks sexual attraction towards others or have a lack of interest in it.

Reddit

This subreddit, r/lgbt, contains posts related to or are about the LGBTQIA+ topic.

Twitter

"#lgbtq" which contains tweets about almost anything related to the LGBTQIA+ community.

LGBTQ+ Representation in the Media

Good representation matters because it can be educational. It affects how people are seen by others and how they feel seen themselves, and that it guides discourse about real people, places, and experiences.

BOOKS
“When he died, all things soft and beautiful and bright would be buried with him.”

The Song of Achilles is a 2011 novel by Madeline Miller. told from the perspective of Achilles’ lover, Patroclus. The novel follows Patroclus' relationship with Achilles, from their initial meeting to their exploits during the Trojan War, with focus on their romantic relationship.
 "I should have gone through life half awake if you'd had the decency to leave me alone"

Maurice is a novel by E. M. Forster written in 1913-1914. Set in early 20th century England, it is a tale of a young man, Maurice Hall, struggling with his sexual orientation in a time when little was known or understood about homosexuality.
"The world isn't binary. Everything isn't black or white, yes or no. Sometimes it's not a switch, it's a dial."

Symptom's of Being a Human is 2016 young adult novel by Jeff Garvin. Riley Cavanaugh is a gender fluid teenager whose sense of gender identity shifts regularly. After moving to a new school and struggling with people's perception of them and being bullied, Riley begins to write an anonymous blog about gender fluidity. While the blog is at first a liberating outlet, things take a turn for the worse when someone claims to know Riley's real identity and threatens to out them.
 "I've learnt some things. Like the way friendship can be just as intense, beautiful and endless as romance. Like the way there's love everywhere around me - there's love for my friends, there's love for my paintings, there's love for myself."

Loveless is a novel by Alice Oseman. Georgia has never been in love, never kissed anyone, never even had a crush – but as a fanfic-obsessed romantic she’s sure she’ll find her person one day. As she starts university Georgia’s ready to find romance. But then she starts to question why love seems so easy for other people but not for her. With new terms thrown at her – asexual, aromantic – Georgia is more uncertain about her feelings than ever.
"Maybe it's better to have gotten it right and been happy for one day instead of living a lifetime of wrongs."

They Both Die at the End is a 2017 novel by Adam Silvera. The story of Mateo and Rufus, two very different guys who live in New York and then, one day at dawn, they receive the call from Death-Cast, a company that alerts people the day they’re going to die.
 "Love may be a grand thing, but goddamn if it doesn't take up more than its fair share of space inside a man"

The Gentleman’s Guide to Vice and Virtue is a story that centres around Lord Henry Montague as his affluent father sends him away on a Grand Tour throughout Europe with his sister and close friend Percy, with hopes that Henry will manage to sober up and pull his weight before beginning work in his father's ranks
"When it comes to true dance, it's not about how you look, it's all about the joy you feel."

Boy Meets Boy focuses on the gay community through a teenager's eyes through a delightfully reversed, pro-gay high school setting. The book is an upbeat story about acceptance and teen love
 "It all seemed to hinge around the fact that I loved the wrong sort of people."

Oranges Are Not the Only Fruit is a story of a young Jeanette, who is raised by an extremely religious mother and their cult of Christianity church. She discovers at a young age that she may be a lesbian but that is strictly forbidden by the church
MOVIES
"It doesn't seem fair that only gay people have to come out. Why is straight the default?"

Everyone deserves a great love story, but for 17-year-old Simon Spier, it's a little more complicated. He hasn't told his family or friends that he's gay, and he doesn't know the identity of the anonymous classmate that he's fallen for online. Resolving both issues proves hilarious, terrifying and life-changing.
 "You were right, I am a homosexual"

Megan considers herself a typical American girl. She excels in school and cheerleading, and she has a handsome football-playing boyfriend, even though she isn't that crazy about him. So she's stunned when her parents decide she's gay and send her to a boot camp meant to alter her sexual orientation. While there, Megan meets a rebellious and unashamed teen lesbian, Graham. Though Megan still feels confused, she starts to have feelings for Graham.
"Do all lovers feel like they're inventing something?"

France, 1770. Marianne, a painter, is commissioned to do the wedding portrait of Héloïse, a young woman who has just left the convent. Héloïse is a reluctant bride to be and Marianne must paint her without her knowing. She observes her by day, to paint her secretly.
 "I'm me"

A look at three defining chapters in the life of Chiron, a young black man growing up in Miami. His epic journey to manhood is guided by the kindness, support and love of the community that helps raise him.
"Don't you know me Kansas City? I'm the new Berlin Wall. Try and tear me down!"

A German emigrant living in a trailer in Kansas is the victim of a botched sex-change operation. Adapted from the critically acclaimed off-Broadway rock theater hit, "Hedwig and the Angry Inch" tells the story of the "internationally ignored" rock singer, Hedwig, and her search for stardom and love.
 "Love is being willing to ruin your good painting for the chance at a great one."

A shy, straight-A Chinese-American student helps the school jock woo a girl whom, secretly, they both desire. They find themselves connecting and learn about the nature of love.
"They lead us like lambs to the slaughter and expect us to just follow. Well, they will see. I am no lamb."

After a series of brutal slayings, a teen and her friends take on an evil force that's plagued their notorious town for centuries
 "Of all the places I could be, I just want to be here with you"

When an interdimensional rupture unravels reality, an unlikely hero must channel her newfound powers to fight bizarre and bewildering dangers from the multiverse as the fate of the world hangs in the balance.
TV SHOWS
"You're my favorite person, I need you to believe me"

Heartstopper is a British coming of age, romance series based on the Webtoon comic and graphic novel by Alice Oseman. It tells the story of Charlie Spring, a gay teenage boy and Nick Nelson who both fell in love after sitting next to each other in school.
 "Don't you get it? I love you. I always have. So please, just this once, stay."

She-Ra, Princess of Power, leads a rebellion to free her land of Etheria from the monstrous invaders the Horde. On the planet Etheria, Adora is an up-and-coming soldier for the Horde eager to defend her home from the menace of the Princesses she has been taught all her life to fear.
"Don't worry, you always have a way of sneaking into people's hearts."

The Owl House centers on Luz Noceda, a human girl who accidentally stumbles upon a portal to the Demon Realm, where she arrives at the Boiling Isles and befriends the rebellious witch Eda Clawthorne and King. Despite not having magical abilities, Luz pursues her dream of becoming a witch by serving as Eda's apprentice at the Owl House and ultimately finds a new family in an unlikely setting.
"I like the wine and not the label. Does that make sense?"

When filthy-rich video store magnate Johnny Rose and his family suddenly find themselves broke, they are forced to leave their pampered lives to regroup and rebuild their empire from within the rural city limits of their only remaining asset - Schitt's Creek, an armpit of a town they once bought as a joke.
"Every time someone steps up and says who they are, the world becomes a better, more interesting place."

Set in the fictional 99th Precinct of the New York City Police Department in Brooklyn, Brooklyn Nine-Nine follows a team of detectives headed by the serious and intellectual Captain Raymond Holt, who is assigned as their new commanding officer in the pilot episode.
 "Love at first sight doesn’t exist. Love takes time and love takes work."

Steven Universe is set in the fictional town of Beach City, Delmarva, where the Crystal Gems live in an ancient beachside temple and protect humanity from monsters and other threats. The Gems are ageless alien warriors who project female humanoid forms from magical gemstones at the core of their being.
VIDEO GAMES
"The only thing that matters is you're my brother. And the only way to move forward... is to keep looking back."

Tell Me Why is centered on twins Alyson and Tyler Ronan as they reunite after ten years apart. The pair must come to terms with the events of their childhood, specifically the death of their mother Mary-Ann Ronan, as they prepare to sell their childhood home. The twins realize that they do not share the same memories of their past, and must work to find the truth of what happened and how their mother died. Tyler is the first transgender playable character from any major studio,
 "Wherever I end up after this... in whatever reality... all those moments between us were real, and they'll always be ours."

Life is Strange focuses on Max Caulfield, an 18-year-old photography student who discovers that she has the ability to rewind time at any moment, leading her every choice to enact the butterfly effect. The player's actions adjust the narrative as it unfolds, and reshape it once allowed to travel back in time.
"Everyone I have cared for has either died or left me. Everyone - except for you."

The Last of Us is an emotionally gripping story of love, hope, perseverance, and recovery. 20 years after a pandemic has radically changed known civilization, infected humans run wild and survivors are killing each other for food, weapons - whatever they can get their hands on.
 "They are real men. Just like you are"

The story follows a player character known as the Inquisitor on a journey to settle the civil unrest in the continent of Thedas and close a mysterious tear in the sky called the "Breach", which is unleashing dangerous demons upon the world.

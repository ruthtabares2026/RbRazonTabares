The Discrimination That The LGBTQ+ Community Faces

The LGBTQ+ community faces a lot of discrimination. People discriminate them because they say that "it's against their religion", "it's disgusting", "it's unnatural", and many other excuses for being judgemental. Not only do they have to face discrimination everyday, but they also get less access to the bare human necessities. These include having less access to some medical care or health care, not allowing to enter some places due to discrimination.

How We Can Help Out

There are various things that we can do to help out. There are various things that we can do, a few examples are as follows:

Create and Protect Safe Spaces for the people of the LGBTQ+ Community

- The people of the LGBTQ+ community still face many real dangers for being open about their sexuality. We can create safe spaces by creating physical spaces or making an inclusive environment for them in everyday places.

Advocate for Legal Changes

- Spreading the word won't be enough. Advocating or publicly supporting Legal Changes in laws or in the community in general that can benefit the LGBTQ+ community can make better changes.

Do Not Let Slurs Slide

- Language is what we use to have a conversation with others. It's how we define ourselves and the world around us. But language can also be used to insult other people or bring other people down. Not letting slurs slide can positively affect a lot of people especially the LGBTQ+ community. Informing other people about not using slurs not only benefit the LGBTQ+ community but also benefit them since they are informed on which is wrong and which is right.

Do Not Make Assumptions about Anyone Else's Sexuality

- Assuming what someone else's gender identity or sexual orientation can, obviously, lead to many misunderstandings. No matter anyone's gender identity, our physical body and clothing should never dictate who we are as a person or how we are supposed to be addressed.

Support Your Family and Friends Who Question Their Sexuality or Identify as LGBTQ+

- If a friend or family member has come out to you, it is likely that it took a lot of strength for them to come out. Them having your support is one of the best ways for you to help out not only your friends and family, but also the LGBTQ+ community.

Do Not Try To Change Their Sexuality

- A lot people go through a long process of figuring out their sexuality or gender identity. While it may be a bit confusing for some people to understand this, an important thing to understand is that these traits are a part of a person's identity and can no longer be changed.
